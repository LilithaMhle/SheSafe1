What is SheSafe?

SheSafe helps South African users make informed choices about menstrual products by providing safety scores, ingredient information, and research-backed guidance.

⚗️ What are EDCs?

Endocrine-Disrupting Chemicals (EDCs) are substances that interfere with the body's hormonal system. The 2026 University of the Free State (UFS) study found EDCs including bisphenols, phthalates, and parabens in commonly used SA menstrual products.

📊 How Safety Scores Work

85–100Very Low Risk

Organic certified, no synthetic chemicals, independently tested.

65–84Low to Moderate Risk

Better ingredient practices, limited concerning chemicals detected.

50–64Moderate Risk

Some concerning ingredients; conventional materials with limited transparency.

0–49Higher Risk

Named in NCC investigation or contains fragrance / known EDC sources.

✅ What to Look For

  • GOTS Certified — Global Organic Textile Standard for cotton
  • OEKO-TEX Standard 100 — Tested against 1000+ harmful substances
  • No added fragrance — Fragrance often contains undisclosed compounds
  • Chlorine-free bleaching — Avoids dioxin production
  • Organic cotton — Grown without synthetic pesticides

⚠️ Red Flags

  • Added fragrance or perfume
  • No ingredient disclosure
  • Conventional chlorine bleaching
  • Named in the 2026 NCC/UFS investigation
